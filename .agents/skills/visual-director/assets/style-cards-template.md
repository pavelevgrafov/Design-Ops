# Style cards — taste calibration deck (8–10 cards)
<!-- Used when the user has opinions but no references. Show cards, ask only:
     "Which 2–3 feel closest? Which are definitely not?" No why-questions. -->

How to build: **micro-renders**, not screenshots of other people's sites.
Each card is a small self-contained HTML composition (≈400×300) containing the
same fake content (a product name, 2 lines of copy, 1 button) rendered with
ONE style's typography + color + surface. Number them 1..N. Never label cards
with style names — labels prime verbal rationalization.

Deck composition (span the boldness band around contract visual_boldness):

| # | Composition | Type voice | Color | Surface |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | centered, airy, symmetric | geometric sans, modest scale | muted monochrome + 1 accent | flat, hairline rules |
| 2 | asymmetric 12-col, dense | serif display + sans body | warm paper + ink | flat, editorial rules |
| 3 | brutalist blocks, full-bleed | mono + condensed display | high-contrast duotone | hard borders, no radius |
| 4 | card grid, generous radius | rounded sans, friendly | soft pastel multi-accent | layered soft shadows |
| 5 | editorial magazine, oversized numerals | high-contrast serif | restrained 2-color | whitespace as surface |
| 6 | dark canvas, spotlight sections | neo-grotesque, tight tracking | dark + electric accent | subtle glow, no blur |
| 7 | retro-grid, framed panels | slab + sans | heritage print palette | bordered frames, stamps |
| 8 | experimental overlap/broken grid | variable font extremes | controlled clashes | mixed flat/cutout |
| 9 | category-conventional reference | category-typical | category-typical | category-typical |
| 10 | wildcard: outside the band, one strong idea | free | free | free |

Rules:
- Cards 1–8 span conventional → challenger; card 9 = the category anchor;
  card 10 = wildcard (full mode only).
- Record the result in the contract:
  `style_card_result: "liked: 2,5,9; rejected: 3,8"`.
- Map liked cards → axes → direction seeds. Rejected cards → anti_references.
